## CLINICAL CRITERIA BASIS

Silversummit does not publish clear clinical criteria for sleep studies.
To prevent denials, this document applies **Carelon-level criteria** (the strictest
in our system) as the conservative baseline. If documentation meets Carelon standards,
it will meet any criteria Silversummit eventually enforces.

**Source:** Carelon Behavioral Health clinical criteria (strictest available)
**Rationale:** Over-documenting never causes denials. Under-documenting does.

## 1. HOME SLEEP APNEA TESTING (HST) CRITERIA

**No auth required, but documentation should still meet these standards.**

### Approved When ANY of the Following:

**A. Observed Apneas**
- Observed apneas during sleep (by bed partner or caregiver)

**B. Combination of TWO of 5 Symptoms:**
1. Excessive daytime sleepiness (ESS >10, inappropriate napping, sleepiness interfering with activities)
2. Habitual snoring or gasping/choking with awakenings
3. Treatment-resistant hypertension (3+ antihypertensive medications)
4. Obesity (BMI >30 or neck circumference >17" men / >16" women)
5. Craniofacial/upper airway abnormalities (adenotonsillar hypertrophy, neuromuscular disease)

**C. Cardiovascular History + ONE Symptom:**
- History of stroke (>30 days prior), TIA, CAD, or sustained arrhythmias
- PLUS at least ONE of the 5 symptoms above

**D. Unexplained Conditions Suggesting OSA:**
- Right heart failure (etiology unclear)
- Polycythemia (etiology unclear)
- Sustained arrhythmia occurring solely during sleep
- Pulmonary hypertension (etiology unclear)

### Follow-up HST Approved When:
- ONE occasion after upper airway surgery for OSA
- ONE occasion after initiation of oral appliance
- Significant weight loss (>=10% body weight) since last study
- Prior to hypoglossal nerve stimulator (if no study within 18 months)

## 2. CONTRAINDICATIONS TO HST

**HST NOT allowed when ANY of:**

| Contraindication | Criteria |
|------------------|----------|
| **Age** | <=17 years (pediatric -> PSG only) |
| **Moderate-Severe COPD** | FEV1/FVC <=0.7 AND FEV1 <80% predicted |
| **Moderate-Severe CHF** | NYHA Class III or IV |
| **CHF + Arrhythmia** | CHF with history of VF or sustained VT (without ICD) |
| **Cognitive Impairment** | Unable to follow instructions, no one to assist |
| **Physical Impairment** | Unable to apply equipment, no one to assist |
| **Suspected CSA** | Central sleep apnea suspected |
| **Suspected Narcolepsy** | Need MSLT |
| **Suspected Idiopathic Hypersomnia** | Need PSG/MSLT |
| **Parasomnia** | Except bruxism and sleep talking |
| **Nocturnal Seizures** | Need attended monitoring |
| **Suspected PLMD** | See criteria below |
| **Failed HST** | Two nights attempted, still suboptimal OR negative with ongoing suspicion |
| **Oxygen Dependent** | Any reason |
| **Recent Stroke** | Within 30 days |
| **Chronic Opioid Use** | When discontinuation not an option |
| **OHS High-Risk** | BMI >33 + bicarbonate >28 mmol/L |
| **Established OHS** | BMI >30 + hypoventilation documented |

### PLMD Criteria (for PSG instead of HST)
To support PLMD suspicion, must document ONE of:
- Pregnancy
- Renal failure
- Iron deficiency anemia
- Peripheral neuropathy
- Use of antidepressant or antipsychotic medications

## 3. IN-LAB PSG CRITERIA (Adults >=18)

### Suspected OSA (with HST Contraindication)
Same symptom criteria as HST, BUT patient has a contraindication to home testing.

### Suspected Sleep Disorder OTHER Than OSA
PSG indicated when suspicion of:

| Condition | Supporting Documentation Required |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| **Central Sleep Apnea** | Heart failure, stroke <=90 days, chronic opioid use, OR Chiari malformation |
| **Narcolepsy** | EDS present >=8 weeks + cataplexy/hallucinations/sleep paralysis |
| **Nocturnal Seizures** | Clinical suspicion |
| **Parasomnia** | Likely to result in harm to patient or others |
| **Idiopathic Hypersomnia** | EDS >=8 weeks, OSA excluded/treated |
| **PLMD** | Document qualifying condition (see above) + OSA/RLS treated first |
| **Nocturnal Desaturation** | Due to severe COPD or restrictive thoracic disorders |

### Follow-up In-Lab PSG
- After upper airway surgery (with HST contraindication)
- After oral appliance initiation (with HST contraindication)
- Weight loss >=10% (with HST contraindication)
- Prior to hypoglossal nerve stimulator (no study within 18 months)
- ONE time after hypoglossal or phrenic nerve stimulator insertion (optimize settings)
- To titrate CPAP/BPAP when APAP contraindicated or failed
- Retitrate CPAP/BPAP when symptoms persist despite compliance (4 hrs/night, 70% nights)

## 4. PEDIATRIC CRITERIA (Age <=17)

### [!] HST NOT ALLOWED -- PSG Required

### PSG Indicated When:
- Habitual snoring + ONE of:
  - Restless/disturbed sleep
  - Behavioral disturbance, ADHD, learning disorders
  - Frequent awakenings
  - Enuresis (bedwetting)
  - Growth retardation/failure to thrive
- Excessive daytime somnolence not explained by other conditions
- Polycythemia not explained
- Cor pulmonale not explained
- Witnessed apnea (>2 respiratory cycles)
- Labored breathing during sleep
- Tonsillar/adenoid hypertrophy in surgical-risk patient (to avoid surgery if no OSA)
- Suspected congenital central hypoventilation
- Sleep-related hypoventilation (neuromuscular disease, chest wall deformity)
- Apparent life-threatening event in infant
- Post-adenotonsillectomy (>8 weeks) if symptoms persist

### Pediatric Follow-up PSG
- Persistent symptoms despite PAP compliance
- Post-adenotonsillectomy >8 weeks
- Prior to hypoglossal nerve stimulator
- Weight loss >=10%
- Titrate CPAP/BPAP after diagnostic confirms candidacy
- Clinical status change or assess therapy efficacy

---

## 5. SPLIT NIGHT CRITERIA

### Technical Requirements
```
SPLIT NIGHT ALLOWED when:
|-- AHI >20/hr during first 2 hours of diagnostic
+-- At least 3 hours remaining for titration
```

### If Split Not Achieved
- Night 1: Diagnostic PSG (95810)
- Night 2: PAP Titration (95811)

---

## 6. TITRATION CRITERIA

### CPAP Indicated (Adults >=18) When BOTH:
1. Sleep study shows:
   - AHI >=15, OR
   - AHI 5-14 with symptoms (EDS, impaired cognition, mood disorders, insomnia, HTN, IHD, stroke history)
2. Appropriate CPAP level has been determined

### CPAP Indicated (Pediatric <=17) When BOTH:
1. Lab-based PSG shows AHI >=1 with appropriate titration
2. ONE of:
   - Adenotonsillectomy unsuccessful
   - Minimal adenotonsillar tissue
   - OSA due to other cause (craniofacial, morbid obesity)
   - Adenotonsillectomy contraindicated

### APAP Allowed When:
- Same criteria as CPAP
- NO contraindications to APAP (see below)

---

## 7. APAP CONTRAINDICATIONS

**APAP NOT allowed when ANY of:**

| Contraindication | Notes |
|------------------|-------|
| CHF | Any |
| Moderate-Severe COPD | FEV1/FVC <=0.7 AND FEV1 <80% predicted |
| Chronic Opioid Use | Any |
| 24-Hour Supplemental O2 | Any |
| Central Sleep Apnea | >=50% central events OR >5 central events/hr |
| Neuromuscular Disorders | Muscular dystrophy, myasthenia gravis, etc. |
| Obesity Hypoventilation Syndrome | BMI >30 + documented hypoventilation |

---

## 8. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

### Definition (CMS Standard)
- PAP use >=4 hours/night on 70% of nights during consecutive 30-day period

### Demonstration Required
- Every 90 days for first year
- Annually thereafter
- NOT required for pediatric patients or BPAP for non-OSA conditions

### Alternative Compliance
- Treating provider (NOT DME) attests clinical benefit at current usage levels

---

## 9. KEY THRESHOLDS SUMMARY

| Metric | Value | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| AHI >=15 | Moderate-Severe OSA | Qualifies for PAP treatment |
| AHI 5-14 + symptoms | Mild OSA | Qualifies if symptoms/comorbids documented |
| AHI >20 in 2 hours | Split eligible | Can proceed to titration |
| >=3 hours | Titration minimum | Required for split-night |
| AHI >=1 (pediatric) | Pediatric OSA | Qualifies for treatment |
| Weight loss >=10% | Significant | Warrants repeat testing |
| PAP 4 hrs/70% nights | Compliance | CMS definition |
| COPD: FEV1 <80% + ratio <=0.7 | Moderate-Severe | Contraindication to HST/APAP |
| CHF: NYHA III-IV | Moderate-Severe | Contraindication to HST/APAP |
| BMI >33 + bicarb >28 | OHS risk | Contraindication to HST |
| EDS >=8 weeks | MSLT prerequisite | Required for narcolepsy/IH workup |
