[OK] PIN-6 RESOLVED -- Repeat Titration Rules

Two distinct paths for existing PAP patients:

PATH A -- STRAIGHT TO RETITRATION (no new diagnostic):
Patient still positive (diagnosis not in question). Needs pressure adjustment.
* Pressure complications with newly obtained equipment
* Weight gain -> pressure may be too low
* Weight loss (moderate) -> pressure may be too high
* Feels less effective / not working as well
* Pressure feels too high
* Within 5 years of original diagnostic study
-> Order: 95811 (titration) or split night
-> Prior diagnostic NOT required to be repeated

PATH B -- REPEAT DIAGNOSTIC FIRST:
Questioning whether patient is still positive at all.
* Significant weight loss (enough to question OSA persistence)
* Corrective surgery (adenotonsillectomy, bariatric, jaw surgery, etc.)
* Any change that could eliminate the underlying condition
-> Order: 95810 (diagnostic PSG) or HST first
-> If still positive -> then titration
-> If negative -> no titration needed

5-YEAR RULE: Measured from original diagnostic study date. After 5 years, repeat diagnostic required regardless of path.

Key distinction: "Do my settings need adjusting?" (Path A) vs. "Do I still have this?" (Path B)
